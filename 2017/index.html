詳細

details

Hackathons have become kind of lame and have lost their original purpose. There's always a promotion being made, gimicky prizes, judging rounds, recruiters, and never enough internet.

So last year we held a dumbathon in Portland. The purpose was to make something, anything. Something dumb, fun, and pointless. Something that will never make a cent for anyone, but will make everyone smile and say neat nonetheless. You know, what hackathons were always supposed to be.

The rules of a dumbathon are simple:

  1. Everyone's invited
  2. No recruiting or product promotion
  3. Make whatever you damn well please

This year we're keeping things spicy and moving the party to Seattle! Labor day weekend worked out pretty well last year so we're sticking with that, and will get a house for the nights of September 1-3, (that's Friday, Saturday, and Sunday with Labor Day on Monday.)

For even more fuego spice we're going to actually have presentations this time! Sunday night we'll hang out and anyone who wants to show off their broken project to everyone else can do it. It's original ideas like this that really put Dumbathons streets ahead of any other hackathon.
